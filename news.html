News

We are sorry to announce that Cristof Koch will not be able to attend the conference as previously announced. We like to share his own words:

We are extremely delighted to announce that, along with the other scheduled keynotes (David Chalmers and Sir Roger Penrose), Anil Seth has just joined the TSC2023. Overcoming a series of logistical difficulties that make the mythological figures of Scylla and Charybdis seem insignificant, Anil Seth will give his keynote lecture “From beast machines to dreamachines”, Tuesday 23rd 11:15 am.
We would like to express our gratitude once again to Anil for his willingness to join us. Welcome on board!
Anil Seth is Professor of Cognitive and Computational Neuroscience and Co-Director of the Centre for Consciousness Science at the University of Sussex. He is also Co-Director of the Canadian Institute for Advanced Research Program on Brain, Mind and Consciousness, a European Research Council Advanced Investigator, and Editor-in-Chief of the journal Neuroscience of Consciousness. He has published more than 200 research papers and is a Clarivate Highly Cited Researcher (2019-2022). His 2017 TED talk has been viewed more than thirteen million times, and his 2021 book Being You: A New Science of Consciousness was an instant Sunday Times Bestseller and a Book of the Year for The Economist, The New Statesman, Bloomberg Business, The Guardian, The Financial Times and elsewhere. I am terribly sorry but I will have to cancel my participation in the Sicily conference. After ten years as the Chief Scientist and then the President of the ‘Allen Institute for Brain Science’ in Seattle, I will be stepping down from my executive position at the end of May, returning to a simpler life as a humble scientist, a development I’m quite happy about. I had not realized that this change of the guard at the Institute goes hand-in-hand with all sorts of meetings with affected personnel, HR, and AllStaff announcements. It is during this time that the Sicily Conference takes place, a conference at which I looked forward to catching up with many friends and colleagues in our community.

I look forward to meeting with you all in 2024 in Tucson.

Christof Koch

April 6th, 2023
